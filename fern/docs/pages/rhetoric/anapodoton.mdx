Also anantapodoton; a kind of ellipsis (q.v.) in
which an entire clause, usually the second member of a correlative expression, is left unstated.
When Jefferson writes "That to secure rights,
Governments are instituted among Men, deriving their just powers from the governed," it is
obvious from what he has already written that
he intends the reader to supply "Wehold self-
evident . . . " Likewise, when Caligula states,
"Would that the Roman people had a single
neck," the unstated concluding clause is self-
evident.
Arthur Quinn and Lyon Rathbun
University of California, Berkeley

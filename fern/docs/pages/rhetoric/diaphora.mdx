title: "Diaphora"
description: "'Dislocation, difference, disagreement'; repetition of a common noun to signify both an individual and that individual's associated qualities."
---

"Dislocation, difference, disagreement"; repetition of a common noun to signify both an individual and that individual's associated qualities.
Peacham illustrates the figure with the following
example: "What man is there living, that would
not have pitied that case if he had been a man." He
points out that in the repetition, man is usedto
signify humanity, "or compassion proper to man's
nature" (The Garden of Eloquence). Shakespeare
is also using man in a diaphora when Alcibiades
asks Timon, "Is man so hateful to thee That art
thyself a man?" (Timon of Athens 4.3.51).

Ellipsis
The omission of a word or phrase easily
understood by the context. Shakespeare used
the figure frequently to achieve a sense of
compression: "Haply you shall not see me
more; or if, a mangled shadow" (Antony and
Cleopatra 4.2). Ellipsis can also be used to
create a telegraphic style, such as "Mother
deceased. Burial tomorrow. Sincerely yours"
(Camus, L'Etranger). Used in interior monologue, ellipses can dramatically suggest
a character's anxiety or impatience: "AmbuBernanos, Oeuvres romanesques). Caesar's last words in Shakespeare's play, "Et tu,
Brute," or Poe's refrain, "only this, and nothing more," are absolute ellipses, whichrequire the reader to supply the omitted material without local textual clues.

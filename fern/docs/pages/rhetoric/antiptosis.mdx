---
title: "Antiptosis"
description: "'Exchange of case.' Whereas the hendiadys (q.v.)"
---

"Exchange of case." Whereas the hendiadys (q.v.)
adds an and between the old and new nouns, the
antiptosis adds an of. The glorious kingdom becomes "the kingdom of glory" rather than "the
kingdom and the glory." The following three examples show how substituting the preposition of
rather than the conjunction and adds weight to the
word being emphasized: "Why are thou so far
from helping me, and from the words of my roaring?" (Psalm 22:1); "And above all these things
put on charity, which is the bond of perfectness"
(Colossians 1:17); "The King's name is a tower of
strength" (Richard III 53.12).

University of California, Berkeley

The substitution of onepart of speech for another. A form of enallage (q.v.), anthimeria is
found in many of Shakespeare's most memorable lines: "Report That I am sudden sick.
Quick and return!" (Antony & Cleopatra
1.3.4); "I'll unhair thy head!" (2.5.64). "His
complexion is perfect gallows" (The Tempest
1.1.32). In these lines Shakespeare has used an
adjective as an adverb, a noun as a verb, and
a noun as an adjective. Many notable linesof
modern poetry are also the result of
anthimeria: "The hot of him ispurest in the
heart" (Stevens); "he sang his didn't he danced
his did" (Cummings); "O dark dark dark.
They all go into the dark. / The vacant interstellar spaces, the vacant into the vacant"
(Eliot).

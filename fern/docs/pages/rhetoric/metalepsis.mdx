A metonymy (q.v.) in which a double substitution seems to have occurred. When Amos
states "And I also have given you cleanness of
teeth in all your cities" (Amos 4:6), he has
associated "cleanness of teeth" with lack of
food, connoting famine. The double substitution in a metalepsis is often used to attribute
a present effect to a remote cause: "The ship
is sinking: damn the wood where the mast
grew. "
Arthur Quinn and Lyon Rathbun
University of California, Berkeley
